7 Elements ~10 Minutes Yours to Keep No Right Answers
Element 01 of 07
Position
where you are
What This Means

Before you navigate anywhere, you need an honest read of where you stand right now. Not where you want to be — where you actually are.

Position is your current financial snapshot: what you own, what you owe, what's coming in, what's going out. No judgment. Just a clear-eyed look at the starting line.

Consider
  • Do I have a clear picture of what I own and owe?
  • Do I know my monthly cash flow — in and out?
  • Have I ever mapped my full financial position on paper?
  • What feels unclear or untracked right now?

Element 02 of 07
Terrain
what surrounds you
What This Means

Terrain is the landscape you're operating in — the financial environment around your life. Tax rules, interest rates, insurance markets, employer benefits. You didn't design these conditions, but they're acting on you regardless.

Understanding terrain doesn't mean mastering it. It means knowing what's out there so you're not caught off guard by things you could have seen coming.

Consider
  • Do I understand the basic tax rules that affect me?
  • Am I aware of the benefits available through my employer?
  • Do I know how interest rates affect my debts or savings?
  • What parts of the financial landscape feel foreign?

Element 03 of 07
Forces
what's acting on you
What This Means

Forces are the pressures, obligations, and dynamics actively shaping your financial life — whether you chose them or not. Debt payments. Family obligations. A lease. A mortgage. Time itself.

Some forces are visible and understood. Others operate quietly in the background. Naming them is the first step toward managing them — rather than being managed by them.

Consider
  • What financial obligations are non-negotiable right now?
  • Are there commitments I've made that I don't fully understand?
  • Who or what has the most pull on my financial decisions?
  • What forces am I ignoring that I probably shouldn't be?

Element 04 of 07
Questions
what matters now
What This Means

The questions you carry are data. What you're unsure about, what keeps surfacing, what you've been meaning to figure out — these aren't signs of failure. They're the map of where orientation is needed most.

Getting your real questions on the table is one of the most valuable things you can do. It focuses the work. It tells your caddy where to look. It turns vague unease into actionable inquiry.

Consider
  • What financial question have I put off the longest?
  • What am I agreeing to that I don't fully understand?
  • What decision is coming that I feel underprepared for?
  • What would I most want explained in plain language?

Element 05 of 07
Pace
where tempo ties in
What This Means

Financial life has rhythms — monthly cash flow, annual taxes, decade-long savings horizons, once-in-a-generation decisions. Pace is about understanding the timing of things.

Moving too fast invites mistakes. Moving too slow invites costs. Knowing your pace — and the pace of what surrounds you — lets you act when action is right, not just when pressure mounts.

Consider
  • Am I making decisions reactively — or deliberately?
  • What decisions feel urgent that might actually have more time?
  • What have I delayed that's actually time-sensitive?
  • Do I know the rhythm of my own financial calendar?

Element 06 of 07
Options
how to scan for lines
What This Means

A good caddy scans the course before recommending a line. Options are the paths actually available to you — the choices you have, even when they're not obvious.

Many people proceed as if they have fewer options than they do — because no one walked them through what's available. Orientation surfaces those options. It doesn't choose for you. It makes sure you're choosing from a full menu, not a partial one.

Consider
  • In my biggest financial decisions — did I know all my options?
  • Where am I proceeding by default rather than by choice?
  • What alternatives haven't I explored because I didn't know they existed?
  • Where do I feel stuck with no good path forward?

Element 07 of 07
Readiness
when action flows
What This Means

Readiness isn't urgency. It's the quiet signal that you're properly equipped and aligned — that it's the right time to proceed.

When you've worked through Position, Terrain, Forces, Questions, Pace, and Options — readiness emerges naturally. Not as pressure. As clarity. This is the point where deliberate action becomes possible. Where you stop reacting and start deciding.

Consider
  • What would "ready" actually feel like for me?
  • What's the one thing standing between me and forward movement?
  • What do I need to understand before I can act with confidence?
  • Am I waiting for certainty — or am I actually already ready?
